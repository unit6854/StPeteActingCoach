truth over performance. Here's what that means from the perspective of a working

## The camera reads your thoughts

On stage you project to the back row. On camera, the lens catches the smallest,
most honest impulse. Great screen acting is specific, internal, and alive —
often smaller than actors expect, and always truthful.

## Continuity, framing, and hitting your mark

Film and TV are built in pieces. You'll repeat a moment take after take, from
different angles, and it needs to match. Learning to stay spontaneous inside
technical requirements is a skill you can train.

## Why a director's eye matters

Most coaches teach from the actor's chair. A director watches how a performance
will actually *cut together* — what the edit needs, what a casting director is
looking for, and what turns a good read into a booking. That
[director-led coaching](/#services) is what sets this studio apart.

## Building camera confidence

Confidence on camera comes from reps and feedback, not luck. The more you work in
front of a lens with someone who can name exactly what's working, the faster you
grow.
