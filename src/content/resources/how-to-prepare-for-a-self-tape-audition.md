Self-tapes are now the front door to most film and television auditions. The
good news: a strong self-tape is something you can learn to produce
consistently. Here's how to prepare a self-tape audition that actually books.

## Get your framing and background right

Frame a clean medium close-up (chest and head), leave a little headroom, and
keep your background simple and neutral. Nothing behind you should compete with
your face — you are the story.

## Light your face, not the room

Soft, even light on the face reads as "on camera." Face a window or a soft light
source, avoid harsh shadows, and make sure your eyes are lit. Camera confidence
starts with actually being seen.

## Sound matters more than you think

Casting will forgive a modest camera before they forgive bad audio. Record in a
quiet space, get your reader close to the mic, and keep their volume lower than
yours.

## Make strong, specific choices

A self-tape is a performance, not a formality. Know what your character wants,
make a bold choice, and stay available to change moment to moment. This is where
[private coaching](/#services) makes the biggest difference — a second set of
eyes catches what you can't see.

## Do a few takes, then stop

Give casting one or two clean options, not ten. Slate cleanly, label your file
as requested, and send it before the deadline.
